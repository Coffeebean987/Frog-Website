The end of the cycle!

Ah, so you have made it to the end of the line. Frogs may come in various shapes and sizes. For example, the Red-Eyed Tree Frog is about the size of a teacup! Sadly, the Red-Eyed Tree Frogs Natural habitat is shrinking, which is causing damage to its numbers Click here for more about these wonderful frogs! The internet is full of wonders and pictures of many frogs! The frog still has a lot to do as it grows! Here is a list below:

  1. Continue Eating
  2. Find a mate
  3. Reside in their current location or find a new home
  4. Watch out for predators that may want to eat it (If it does not have any defenses)

Many other challenges may arise for the frog, but is up to nature to decide whether or not it will let the frog live, or die. Thankyou for visiting the website and I hope you enjoyed your time here! Please feel free to email us anytime! We appreciate any responses or comments!
