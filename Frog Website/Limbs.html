The growth and development of the frog

The frog, like humans, go through many body changes, such as an extra set of front legs, and the loss of their tail. Above is an example of these very changes. The picture above shows the complete changes a frog goes through, from egg to adult. There are many books about frogs out there for kids! There are also books for adults, such as this one here.

Down below is a table, showing a few various types of frogs and whether they are dangerous or not. Remember, not all frogs are cute and friendly, some may pack a bite, or toxins/poisons that may kill you!

Frog Can kill? Notes
Golden Poison Dart Frog Yes Gold in color. Can kill 10 humans with poison.
Lovely Poison Frog Maybe Least poisonous. Do not eat.
Phantasmal Poison Frog Yes Can kill one human with its poison.
Variable Poison Frog Yes Very tiny Do not touch
Blanchard's Cricket Frog No Small, primarily found in Oklahoma and Texas
Rocky Mountain Tailed Frog No Primarily found in Idaho. Black and dark brown color.
Green-striped Frog No Olive in color Found in Australia
